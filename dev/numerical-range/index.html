Numerical range

Definition

For any square matrix $A$ of dimension $d$, one defines its numerical range (also called field of values [( :murnaghan1932field )]) as a subset of the complex plane: $$ W(A)=\{ z \in \mathbb{C}: z=\bra{\psi} A\ket{\psi}, \ \ket{\psi} \in {\mathbb{C}}\^d, \ \braket{\psi}{\psi}=1\}. $$ Following a common convention we denote here the numerical range by $W(X)$, which goes back to the German term \"Wertevorrat\" originally used by Hausdorff [( :hausdorff1919wertvorrat )].
