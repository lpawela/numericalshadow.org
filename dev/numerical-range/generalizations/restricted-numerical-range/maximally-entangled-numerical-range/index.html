Maximally entangled numerical range

Definition

Maximally entangled numerical range $W\^{\mathrm{ent}}(A)$ of a square matrix $A$ of size $d = d1 \times d2$ is a subset of the complex plane defined as

$$ W\^{\mathrm{ent}}(A)=\{z \in \mathbb{C}:\ z= \bra{\psi}A\ket{\psi},\ \ket{\psi}\in\mathbb{C}\^{d1\times d2}_\mathrm{ent} \}. $$

$\mathbb{C}\^{d1\times d2}_\mathrm{ent}= \{ \ket{\psi} : \ket{\psi}=(U1\otimes U2)\sum_{i=1}\^{d_\min} \frac{1}{\sqrt{d_\min}} \ket{\psii\^1}\otimes \ket{\psii\^2} \} $, where

  • $d_\min={\min(d1,d2)}$,
  • $\ket{\psii\^1}$, $\ket{\psii\^2}$ form orthonormal bases in $\mathbb{C}\^{d1}$ and $\mathbb{C}\^{d2}$ respectively,
  • $U1\in SU(d1)$ and $U2\in SU(d2)$.
