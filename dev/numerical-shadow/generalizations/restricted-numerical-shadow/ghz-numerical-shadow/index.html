GHZ numerical shadow

GHZ entangled numerical shadow of a matrix $A$ of dimension $d$ is defined as a probability ditribution $PA(z)$ on the complex plane, supported on the maximally entangled numerical range $W\^\mathrm{ent}(A)$. $$ PA(z) := \int_{\Omega} {\rm d} \mu(\psi) \delta\Bigl( z-\langle \psi\|A\|\psi\rangle\Bigr), $$ where $\mu(\psi)$ denotes the unique unitarily invariant (Fubini-Study) measure on the set $$ \Omega=\{\ket{\psi} \in \mathbb{C}\^{2\^d}: \ket{\psi} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \bigotimes_{i=1}\^d Ui \left( \ket{0}\^{\otimes d} + \ket{1}\^{\otimes d} \right)\}, $$ where $Ui \in SU(2)$

Example

GHZ entangled numerical shadow of a unitary matrix [(:puchala2012restricted)] $$ U=\text{diag}\left( 1,e\^{\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, e\^{\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, e\^{-\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, e\^{\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, e\^{-\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, e\^{-\frac{2 \ii \pi }{3}}, 1 \right) $$
